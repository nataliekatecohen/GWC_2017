Building Your Confidence

Connect with others who share the same positive thinking and approach to life. Surround yourself with individuals who share the same values of body positivity and acceptance as you do. Sometimes, connecting with like minds can make you feel accepted in ways you may not have realized before.

Wear yellow on days you aren’t feeling confident. Yellow has been shown to improve mood and calm energy.

Turn on some fun music - dance and/or sing along. Music can make you feel more powerful, and is heavily linked with mood.

Stand tall, with your back straight. Posture is an outer sign of confidence, and sometimes, you just have to fake it ‘til you make it.

Stretch or do some yoga whenever you have a little free time. Physicality can make you feel strong, and moving around can improve your mood much more than vegging out on the couch watching Netflix.

Think About:

1) You pass thousands of people every day: on the street, on the subway, at work or school. At least one of those people has thought something complimentary about you. Your hair looks great, those pants flatter you really well, your shirt is super nice, your eyes pop spectacularly, etc.

2) More than half of those people won’t even have taken notice of you or whatever you’re uncomfortable about. Everyone is way too wrapped up with being concerned about their own insecurities to judge other people.
